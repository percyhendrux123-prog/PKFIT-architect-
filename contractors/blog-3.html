Spring Landscaping Tips for Louisiana Homeowners

Spring in Louisiana means everything grows fast. Here's how to get ahead of the season and set your property up right.

Louisiana doesn't ease into spring — it arrives fast and humid. One week you're dealing with frost warnings, the next your grass is growing an inch a day and the azaleas are exploding. If you're not ready, your yard gets away from you in a hurry. The good news? A little preparation in late February and March sets you up for a beautiful landscape all the way through fall.

Here's what we recommend for homeowners in Baker and the surrounding area.

1. Clean Up Winter Debris First

Before you plant anything new, clear out the old. Remove fallen branches, dead leaves, and any debris that's accumulated over winter. This isn't just cosmetic — decaying organic matter can harbor mold, fungi, and pests that will attack your plants once the temperature rises. Rake out flower beds, clear gutters, and haul off anything that's been sitting too long.

2. Aerate and Feed Your Lawn

Louisiana soil — especially the heavy clay we have around Baker — gets compacted over winter. Aerating your lawn in early spring opens up the soil so water, air, and nutrients can reach the root zone. Follow up with a slow-release fertilizer appropriate for your grass type (Bermuda, St. Augustine, and Zoysia are all common here). This gives your lawn the fuel it needs to come back thick and green.

3. Mulch Your Beds

A fresh 2-3 inch layer of mulch around trees, shrubs, and flower beds does three critical things: it retains moisture during our hot summers, suppresses weeds before they take hold, and regulates soil temperature during the swings between cool nights and hot days. Use hardwood mulch or pine bark — both work well in our climate. Just keep mulch a few inches away from plant stems and tree trunks to prevent rot.

4. Prune Before New Growth Takes Off

Late winter through early spring is the best time to prune most trees and shrubs in Louisiana. Once new growth starts, you want plants putting energy into healthy branches, not dead or crossing ones. Remove any dead, damaged, or diseased branches first. Then shape for aesthetics and airflow. For flowering shrubs like crepe myrtles — a Baker yard staple — prune before they leaf out so they bloom heavier in summer.

5. Check Your Irrigation System

If you have an irrigation system, spring is the time to inspect it before you need it. Turn each zone on and walk the property. Look for broken heads, clogged nozzles, and leaks. Make sure coverage is even — dry spots in July are a lot harder to fix than wet heads in March. If you don't have irrigation, now is a great time to consider installing one before the summer heat arrives.

6. Plan Your Plantings

Spring is prime planting season in Louisiana. If you're adding color, think about plants that thrive in our heat and humidity: lantana, pentas, hibiscus, and confederate jasmine are all excellent choices. For shade trees, live oaks and bald cypress are long-term investments that do well here. Plan your layout before you buy — consider sun exposure, mature size, and water needs so everything works together.

7. Stay Ahead of Weeds

Weeds in Louisiana don't wait for an invitation. Apply a pre-emergent herbicide in late February or early March to prevent spring weeds before they sprout. Once weeds are visible, you're already behind. For existing weeds, pull them by hand or spot-treat — avoid blanket spraying near plants you want to keep. Consistent mowing at the right height also helps your grass outcompete weeds naturally.

A well-maintained landscape does more than look good — it increases your property value, improves curb appeal, and makes your outdoor space more enjoyable.
