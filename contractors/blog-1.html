5 Signs Your Roof Needs Replacing

Your roof protects everything underneath it. Here's how to know when it's time for a new one — before a storm makes the decision for you.

Most homeowners don't think about their roof until there's a bucket in the living room. But by the time you see water damage inside, the problem outside has been building for months — sometimes years. In Louisiana, where heat, humidity, and hurricane season push roofing materials to their limits, staying ahead of roof failure isn't optional. It's how you protect your home and your wallet.

Here are five signs it's time to call a professional.

1. Your Shingles Are Curling, Cracking, or Missing

Walk your property and look up. If shingles are visibly curled at the edges, cracked down the middle, or missing entirely, your roof's protective barrier is compromised. Curling shingles are a sign of age and moisture damage — they're no longer lying flat, which means water, wind, and debris can get underneath them. Once that happens, the underlayment and decking start taking damage fast.

2. You're Finding Granules in Your Gutters

Those tiny, sandlike particles in your gutters aren't dirt — they're shingle granules, and they're what protects your roof from UV damage. When shingles start shedding granules, it means they're deteriorating. A little shedding on a new roof is normal, but if your roof is more than 10 years old and you're seeing heavy granule loss, it's a warning sign that replacement is approaching.

3. There's Daylight Coming Through Your Attic

Go into your attic on a sunny day and turn off the lights. If you can see pinpoints of light coming through the roof boards, water can get through those same spots. This is one of the clearest indicators of structural damage. If you see light, you almost certainly have — or will soon have — leaks.

4. Your Roof Is 20+ Years Old

Most asphalt shingle roofs are rated for 20 to 25 years under normal conditions. In Louisiana, the combination of intense sun, heavy rain, and high humidity can shorten that lifespan significantly. If your roof is approaching 20 years old, even if it looks fine from the ground, it's smart to get a professional inspection. Many problems aren't visible without getting up on the roof.

5. Your Energy Bills Are Climbing

A failing roof lets more heat into your home during summer and lets conditioned air escape. If your energy bills have been creeping up without a clear explanation, your roof's insulation and ventilation may be compromised. A new roof with proper ventilation can make a noticeable difference in your monthly utility costs — especially during Louisiana summers.
