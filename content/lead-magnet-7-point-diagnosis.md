# The 7-Point Diagnosis

**Find the exact reason you're stuck — in 15 minutes.**

*A PKFIT Self-Assessment. Phase 1 of The Blueprint, compressed.*

---

## Before You Begin

You already know something is wrong. You just don't know exactly what.

Most men fail their own bodies not because they lack discipline — but because they're fixing the wrong thing. They're working hard on what they *think* the problem is, while the actual problem quietly keeps them stuck.

This diagnosis doesn't fix anything. It does something more valuable: it tells you *exactly what's broken,* so you stop wasting months on the wrong fix.

**How it works:**
- 7 diagnostic categories. Each one maps to a pillar of body recomposition.
- 3 honest yes/no questions per category.
- Score each category 0–3 (one point per "yes").
- Total score at the end: 0–21.
- Your score tells you where you are. The verdict tells you what to do next.

**The only rule:** If you lie to yourself on any question, the diagnosis is worthless. Answer like nobody is watching — because nobody is.

Pen. Paper. 15 minutes. Go.

---

## 1. TRAINING STRUCTURE

Are you training — or are you *going to the gym*?

**Q1.** If someone asked you right now what this week's training plan is, could you answer without looking at your phone?

**Q2.** Is your current training program written down with exact sets, reps, and progression targets — not just "chest day, back day"?

**Q3.** Have you increased weight, reps, or intensity on at least one lift in the last 14 days?

**Score: ___/3**

*What your score means here:*
- **0–1:** You don't have a training program. You have a gym habit. There is a difference, and that difference is your entire plateau.
- **2:** You have structure but no progression. You're practicing, not training.
- **3:** Structure is locked. This isn't your problem.

---

## 2. NUTRITION INTELLIGENCE

Do you know what you're eating — or are you guessing and hoping?

**Q1.** Could you tell me, within 200 calories, what you ate yesterday?

**Q2.** Do you know your approximate daily protein target — and do you hit it at least 5 days out of 7?

**Q3.** Have you eaten a meal in the last 48 hours that you knew was off-plan *as you were eating it*?

*(Score a point for NO on Q3 — this one is reversed.)*

**Score: ___/3**

*What your score means here:*
- **0–1:** You are not eating "clean." You are eating *reactively.* No amount of gym work will outrun this.
- **2:** You have awareness without discipline. The knowing isn't the problem. The doing is.
- **3:** Your kitchen is a weapon. This isn't your problem.

---

## 3. RECOVERY ARCHITECTURE

Is your recovery a system — or an accident?

**Q1.** Did you sleep 7+ hours last night?

**Q2.** In the last 7 days, has your sleep been within the same 60-minute window (e.g., always between 10:30–11:30 PM)?

**Q3.** Do you have a non-negotiable wind-down ritual — one that happens every night regardless of how busy the day was?

**Score: ___/3**

*What your score means here:*
- **0–1:** You cannot build a body on broken sleep. Period. Everything downstream of this is wasted effort.
- **2:** You have the hours but not the rhythm. Your body doesn't trust your schedule — that matters more than you think.
- **3:** You recover like a pro. This isn't your problem.

---

## 4. MOBILITY & IMBALANCE

Is your body moving correctly — or fighting itself every rep?

**Q1.** Can you squat below parallel without your heels lifting or your back rounding?

**Q2.** When you stand relaxed in front of a mirror, do both your shoulders sit at the same height?

**Q3.** Do you have any chronic pain or "always tight" area that you've been ignoring for more than 30 days?

*(Score a point for NO on Q3 — this one is reversed.)*

**Score: ___/3**

*What your score means here:*
- **0–1:** You're training around a broken movement pattern. Every session reinforces the imbalance. This is the invisible leak.
- **2:** You have compensations you haven't addressed. They will catch up to you — probably during your best year of training.
- **3:** Your hardware works. This isn't your problem.

---

## 5. IDENTITY ALIGNMENT

Do your daily habits match the man you say you want to be?

**Q1.** If a stranger followed you for 7 days with a camera — would they *guess* you're someone training for a body transformation?

**Q2.** Have you made a choice in the last 48 hours that the person you want to become would be proud of?

**Q3.** Do you describe yourself as "working on it" more than once a week?

*(Score a point for NO on Q3 — this one is reversed.)*

**Score: ___/3**

*What your score means here:*
- **0–1:** You are still negotiating with an identity you've outgrown. Until you stop, no program will stick.
- **2:** You're mid-shift. The new identity is forming but the old one keeps voting.
- **3:** The identity is locked. This isn't your problem.

---

## 6. CONSISTENCY PATTERN

How many days in a row before you break?

**Q1.** What is your current longest streak of hitting your plan (training + nutrition) without a full off-day? *Be exact.*

**Q2.** Have you completed a full calendar month of your current program without missing more than 2 sessions?

**Q3.** When you break a streak, do you restart the next day — or wait for "Monday"?

*(Score a point if you restart the next day, not Monday.)*

**Score: ___/3**

*What your score means here:*
- **0–1:** You're a serial restarter. The restart habit is your actual training program. Until that changes, nothing else can.
- **2:** You can do two weeks. You've never broken the 21-day wall. That wall is where identity lives.
- **3:** You finish what you start. This isn't your problem.

---

## 7. ENVIRONMENTAL DESIGN

Is your daily environment helping — or sabotaging — you?

**Q1.** Is there food in your house right now that, if eaten, would take you off-plan?

*(Score a point for NO.)*

**Q2.** Is your gym bag packed, located where you can grab it on your way out, and ready to go right now?

**Q3.** Do the people you spend the most time with (partner, friends, coworkers) know your goals and respect them?

**Score: ___/3**

*What your score means here:*
- **0–1:** You're trying to win a war your environment is actively losing for you. Willpower against environment is a losing bet every time.
- **2:** You have some friction removed but not enough. The weak spots are where you always fall.
- **3:** Your environment is an ally. This isn't your problem.

## What Your Score Means

### **0–7 points: THE BROKEN FOUNDATION**

You're fighting yourself every day without a system. Every gain you make gets lost on the next bad day because you don't have the infrastructure to hold it.

**Hard truth:** You don't need to train harder. You need to stop running a program on broken hardware. Most men in this range try to fix everything at once — and burn out within 10 days. The only thing that works is fixing *one thing at a time, in the right order.*

**Your next 30 days:** Focus on the lowest-scoring section above. That's your Phase 1. Everything else is downstream.

---

### **8–14 points: THE SCATTERED BUILDER**

You have pieces. You know stuff. You've tried stuff. But nothing is connected.

This is the most frustrating place to be — because you feel like you *should* be further along than you are. And you're not wrong.

**Hard truth:** You don't have a knowledge problem. You have an *integration* problem. Your training doesn't talk to your nutrition. Your nutrition doesn't respect your recovery. Your recovery doesn't match your identity. Every system is running in isolation and none of them are compounding.

**Your next 30 days:** You don't need more pieces. You need a framework that connects the ones you already have. Order matters.

---

### **15–21 points: THE EXECUTION GAP**

That's an advanced score. Most men don't land here — and if you did, I'm going to be direct with you.

**Hard truth:** You don't have a knowledge problem. You don't have a structure problem. You have an *execution* problem. You know what to do. You just don't do it consistently enough, for long enough, to get the result you want.

At your score, the Blueprint isn't about teaching you anything new. It's about closing the last mile — the one that separates "almost" from "actually."

**Your next 30 days:** Stop researching. Stop planning. Run something — anything — for 30 uninterrupted days. Identity shift is your missing piece.
