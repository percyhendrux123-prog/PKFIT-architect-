# PKFIT Reel Scripts — "The Mirror" Collection

A collection of 45–60s reel scripts built to stop the scroll, hit the pain point with precision, hold the viewer through a hard truth, and close with the recognition that *we're still standing in the exact same place.* The goal: drive traffic from reels → PKFIT Blueprint landing page → Gumroad.

All scripts share the same skeleton so they can be delivered with range:

- **HOOK (0–4s):** Mirror line. Stops the scroll. Direct address, uncomfortable specificity.
- **HOLD (4–25s):** Paints the picture so accurately the viewer freezes.
- **HARD TRUTH (25–50s):** The thing they already know but have been refusing to say out loud.
- **CLOSE (50–60s):** The loop — *same place, same body, same excuse* — and the CTA.

Each script is ~130–150 words (≈150 wpm spoken pace). Delivery notes are in [brackets] — beats, tone shifts, eye contact cues.

---

## Delivery Guidance (read first)

- **Voice:** Low, controlled, unhurried. You are not convincing them — you are *diagnosing* them.
- **Eye contact:** Straight into the lens. Don't blink away on the hard lines.
- **Pacing:** Speed up slightly on the HOLD (paint the picture fast). Slow *way* down on the HARD TRUTH. One-beat pauses on every line the viewer needs to sit with.
- **Range:** Scripts 1, 4, 7 are intimate/quiet. Scripts 2, 6, 8 are cerebral/surgical. Scripts 3, 5, 10 are confrontational. Script 9 is prophetic.
- **Environment:** Low light. Gold tone. Mirror or window visible in frame on at least half of them — let the metaphor breathe.
- **CTA card:** On-screen text `THE PKFIT BLUEPRINT — LINK IN BIO` pinned from 50s onward.

## Script 1 — "The Mirror"
*Register: intimate, quiet, devastating*

**HOOK (0–4s):**
"You looked in the mirror this morning. And you lied to yourself. Again."

**HOLD (4–22s):**
"You said 'Monday.' You said 'next week.' You said 'after this weekend.' [beat] You've been saying it since January. You've been saying it since *last* January. And the body looking back at you hasn't changed — because the man behind it hasn't either."

**HARD TRUTH (22–48s):**
"Here's what nobody will tell you. You don't have a discipline problem. You have an identity problem. [beat] You still believe you're the kind of person who *tries.* Not the kind who finishes. And every time you restart on Monday, you prove it to yourself all over again."

**CLOSE (48–60s):**
"A year from now, you'll stand in front of that same mirror. Same body. Same excuses. Same quiet disappointment. [beat] Unless something about the man changes. Not the plan. The man. [pause] Link in bio."

## Script 2 — "Same Room, Same Body"
*Register: cerebral, surgical*

**HOOK (0–4s):**
"There's a guy at your gym. You've seen him for three years. He looks exactly the same."

**HOLD (4–26s):**
"Same shorts. Same routine. Same three sets of bicep curls in the mirror. He shows up. He puts in the time. He *thinks* he's training. [beat] And his body has refused to change for 1,095 days in a row. [beat] You know who I'm describing. Because last week — you caught your reflection and realized it was you."

**HARD TRUTH (26–50s):**
"Consistency without diagnosis is just repetition. You're not plateauing. You're *performing* fitness. [beat] There's a difference between working out and building a body. One feels productive. The other actually produces. And for three years — you've been choosing the one that feels good."

**CLOSE (50–60s):**
"30 days from now, you'll either still be that guy — or you won't. The Blueprint tells you which. Link in bio."

## Script 3 — "The Receipts"
*Register: confrontational, prosecutorial*

**HOOK (0–4s):**
"Open your camera roll. Scroll back six months. Find a shirtless photo."

**HOLD (4–26s):**
"Now look at yourself right now. [beat] Tell me what changed. Not what you *planned* to change. Not what you *meant* to change. What actually changed. [beat] Because I already know the answer. So do you. [beat] That's why you never take the second photo."

**HARD TRUTH (26–50s):**
"You don't have a motivation problem. You have a measurement problem. You've been training in the dark for six months and calling the darkness 'trying.' [beat] If you can't see the progress — there isn't any. And the brutal truth is, you already knew that. You just needed someone to say it out loud."

**CLOSE (50–60s):**
"Six more months of this — or 30 days of something that actually works. Your call. Link in bio."

## Script 4 — "The Story You Tell"
*Register: intimate, slow, uncomfortable*

**HOOK (0–4s):**
"You've been telling people you're 'working on it' for two years."

**HOLD (4–26s):**
"Your partner has heard it. Your friends have heard it. Your coworkers have heard it. [beat] *You've* heard it — every morning, in your own head, in the shower, staring at the ceiling before bed. 'I'm working on it.' [beat] No, you're not. You're *talking* about it. And your body keeps the receipts."

**HARD TRUTH (26–50s):**
"The worst lies we tell are the ones we tell ourselves with the word 'soon.' [beat] Soon becomes someday. Someday becomes never. [beat] And never becomes the shape you die in. [pause] You don't need more motivation. You need a mirror — and a system that refuses to let you keep lying."

**CLOSE (50–60s):**
"30 days. No more 'working on it.' Link in bio."

## Script 5 — "Skinny-Fat"
*Register: confrontational, specific*

**HOOK (0–4s):**
"You're not fat. You're not skinny. That's the problem — and nobody's telling you the truth about it."

**HOLD (4–28s):**
"You eat 'clean.' You hit the gym four days a week. Your shirt hides it well. [beat] But the second you take it off — you see it. That soft, undefined, *nothing* shape. The kind that makes you angle yourself in photos. The kind that makes you wait to post until you're 'a little leaner.' [beat] Except — you never get a little leaner. Do you?"

**HARD TRUTH (28–52s):**
"Skinny-fat isn't a body problem. It's a diagnosis you've been avoiding. You've been training for the body you already have. Not the one you need. [beat] And no amount of clean eating will fix a plan that was wrong from day one."

**CLOSE (52–60s):**
"30 days to fix what you've been ignoring. Link in bio."

## Script 6 — "Monday Man"
*Register: cerebral, wry*

**HOOK (0–4s):**
"Count how many Mondays you've started over."

**HOLD (4–26s):**
"Not weeks. *Mondays.* [beat] The 'this is it' Mondays. The 'new me' Mondays. The 'I'm locked in now' Mondays. [beat] Thirty? Forty? A hundred? [beat] You've restarted more times than most people train. And you're still standing in the same body, on the same Sunday night, planning the same Monday."

**HARD TRUTH (26–50s):**
"Restarting isn't discipline. It's avoidance dressed up as commitment. [beat] Every Monday is a pardon you give yourself for the week you just wasted. [beat] And the reason nothing sticks is simple — you never had a system. You had a *feeling.* Feelings don't build bodies. Frameworks do."

**CLOSE (50–60s):**
"One system. 30 days. No more Mondays. Link in bio."

## Script 7 — "Quiet Disappointment"
*Register: intimate, whispered, slow*

**HOOK (0–4s):**
"Nobody talks about the quiet disappointment of being in your own body."

**HOLD (4–28s):**
"You walk past a window and catch your reflection. It doesn't match the version of you in your head. [beat] So you look away. [beat] You see a shirtless photo from five years ago and flinch. So you scroll past. [beat] Your partner tells you you look fine — and somehow that makes it worse. Because you know. And they know. And everyone is being polite about it."

**HARD TRUTH (28–52s):**
"Being 'fine' is the most dangerous place a man can live. [beat] Fine is how you die slowly. Fine is how a decade passes and you realize you never once felt sharp. [pause] You don't want motivation. You want to stop avoiding windows."

**CLOSE (52–60s):**
"30 days to become someone who doesn't look away. Link in bio."

## Script 8 — "The Diagnosis"
*Register: surgical, direct*

**HOOK (0–4s):**
"Let me diagnose you in 30 seconds. Tell me if I'm wrong."

**HOLD (4–28s):**
"You know more about training than most coaches. You watch the videos. You read the articles. You can argue about volume, frequency, macros. [beat] And your body has refused to reflect any of that knowledge for years. [beat] You've confused information with transformation. You've been studying the map while standing still on the road."

**HARD TRUTH (28–52s):**
"Knowledge without diagnosis is noise. [beat] You don't need another program. You need someone to point at the exact thing you're avoiding. [beat] And I'll tell you what I already know. You know what's wrong. You've just never been forced to face it."

**CLOSE (52–60s):**
"30 days. Diagnosis first. Everything else after. Link in bio."

## Script 9 — "Same Place"
*Register: prophetic, warning*

**HOOK (0–4s):**
"Same body. Same year. Same excuse. Different calendar."

**HOLD (4–26s):**
"It's April. In January, you said — *this year.* In March, you said — *starting Monday.* [beat] It's April. And the man in your mirror is the exact same man who made those promises. [beat] You'll tell yourself summer. You'll tell yourself fall. You'll tell yourself New Year's. And then — [beat] it'll be January again. And we'll be having this exact conversation."

**HARD TRUTH (26–50s):**
"The scariest thing about being stuck isn't that it's painful. It's that it's *comfortable.* [beat] You've made peace with a version of yourself you used to hate. And the longer you stay — the more that man becomes the real you. Not the one you're working toward. The one you actually are."

**CLOSE (50–60s):**
"30 days to stop lying. The Blueprint. Link in bio."

## Script 10 — "The Inheritance"
*Register: confrontational, generational, heavy*

**HOOK (0–4s):**
"Look at a photo of your dad at your age. [beat] That's the body you're on track to inherit."

**HOLD (4–28s):**
"Not because of genetics. Because of patterns. [beat] He didn't plan to look like that at 40 either. Neither did his father. They just — drifted. One skipped workout. One extra year of 'I'll start next month.' One decade of 'fine.' [beat] And you're on the same slide. You just haven't hit the bottom yet."

**HARD TRUTH (28–52s):**
"Bodies don't decline from one big failure. They decline from a thousand small permissions. [beat] And the man you'll be at 50 is being built — right now — by what you refuse to do today."

**CLOSE (52–60s):**
"30 days to break the pattern. Before the pattern becomes you. Link in bio."

## Recommended Shooting Order

If you batch-shoot all 10 in one session, order them by emotional register so you don't whiplash your delivery:

1. Start cerebral (2, 6, 8) — warm up your voice on the surgical ones.
2. Move to confrontational (3, 5, 10) — energy rising.
3. End on intimate (1, 4, 7, 9) — by then your voice will be tired, which is *exactly* what these scripts need.

## Post Cadence

Don't post all 10 in a row. Drop one every 2–3 days. Track which register gets the most saves and shares — that tells you which direction to lean next time. **Saves > likes** for content that actually converts.
